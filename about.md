My name is <b>Taehun Lee</b> (<a href="/download/202007_THLee.pdf" download >résumé</a>), and I am a postdoctoral research assoicate in the department of Chemisty at Princeton University (Supervisor: <a href="https://chemistry.princeton.edu/faculty/annabella-selloni">Prof. Annabella Selloni</a>).

My research interest lies in the theoretical study and design of novel (low-dimensional) oxides (on metal support) at the realistic conditions (e.g., controlled growth conditions, solvation effects, and electrochemical environments) for renewable energy applications (e.g., water splitting reaction and CO<sub>2</sub> utilization). I have co-authored 16 high-profile publications, ranking well within the top-tier scientific journals in chemistry, physics, and materials science. For further information, I refer to those websites (<a href="https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=E4QBWfAAAAAJ&hl=en">Google scholar</A> and <a href="https://orcid.org/0000-0002-5435-5910">ORCID: 0000-0002-5435-5910</A>).

<h3><b>Research experiences and achievements</b></h3>
In the past few years, I have performed density-functional theory (DFT) calculations to understand how local surface and interface geometries modulate their functionalities by exploiting theoretical surface science approaches. It covers an electronic structure analysis, atomistic statistic thermodynamics, and spectroscopy/microscopy simulations. Below are the detailed topics I investigated until now; 

- __Oxidation of (bi)metal surfaces under controlled growth conditions__
- __Water-splitting reactions on oxide surfaces under the water environment__
- __Development and application of theoretical surface characterizations of the surface processes__

After I move to Princeton, I am interested in the introduction of machine learning methods to materials science to understand oxides surface properties in particular under the electrochemical environment for the better performances of the heterogeneous catalytic system.
