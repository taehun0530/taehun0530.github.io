<center><h3> <b>Research aims</b></h3></center>
<ul>
  <li><b> Rationalization of structure−property relationship for the material design</b>: The design of materials with optimal properties can be realized by the <b>examining the correlation between the structural properties</b> (e.g, inter- and intra-distortion of atoms, structural connectivity - polymorphism or polytypism, and structural reconstructions) and <b> electronic structures </b> (or the impact of atomic structural changes to their physiochemical properties). (Related research theme: <u> <a href="/blog/B">polymorphic (polytypic) expression of metal oxide)</A> </u></li>
  <li><b> Investigation of surface processes in atomic scale</b>: I am interested in the <b>understanding of growth mechanisms and heterogeneous catalysis processes </b> which occur at surfaces and interfaces. Despite of receiving a tremendous amount of interest in those surface progresses, the <b> detailed surface mechanisms </b> (at the atomic-scale) are still clearly lacking. Therefore, those theoretical surface characterization approaches are able to contribute to build the firm fundamental foundations, and it can bring positive environmental impact, particularly in the area of clean energy technologies. (Related research theme: <u><a href="/blog/D">Metal oxidation</A></u> phenomenon and <u><a href="/blog/A">theoretical aspect of catalysis</A></u> - surface process mechanistic studies, <u><a href="/blog/A">Theoretical surface characterization</A></u> - spectroscopic and mircoscopic analyses) </li>
</ul><br>

The research conducted in the Materials Theory Group (at Yonsei University, South Korea) aims <b>to understand the structural properties of materials </b> (from metal to oxide, in particularly focusing on surface properties) and <b> their impact to physiochemical properties</b>. And I hope these fundamental knowledges can play a key role to design devices with desired performance in the industrial perspective.<br><br>

Following approaches make it possible to obtain these objectives. The main work horse is the <b>density-functional theory by the combining the general concepts in solid-state physics</b>.<br><br><br>

<center><h3> <b>Theoretical approaches </b></h3></center>
<ul>
  <li><b>Density-functional theory (DFT) </b> and beyond, using <a href="https://www.vasp.at/">Vienna Ab initio Simulation Package (VASP)</A>, DMol<sup>3</sup> </li>
  <li><b><i>Ab initio</i> molecular dynamics (<i>ai</i>MD)</b> which is implemented in VASP code</li>
  <li>Advanced <i>ab initio</i> atomistic <b>thermodynamics</b> and Gibbs-Wulff theorem</li>
  <li>Theoretical <b>core level binding energy (BE) </b> and their shifts computation for the direct comparison with X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS) observation</li>
  <li><b>Scanning tunneling microscopic (STM) simulations</b> via <a href="http://www.phy.bme.hu/~palotas/">bSKAN package</A> -- first-principles simulation program of scanning tunneling microscopic data and spectroscopic spectra, developed by Dr. Krisztián Palotás and his colleagues)</li>
  <li><b>Structural evolution</b> and <b>discovery</b> with nature-inspired algorithms: <a href="http://www.calypso.cn/">CALYPSO</A> (Crystal structure AnaLYsis by Particle Swarm Optimization; Structure prediction software using swarm intelligence) and <a href="http://ann.atomistic.net/">Atomic Energy Network (ænet)</A> </li>
</ul>
