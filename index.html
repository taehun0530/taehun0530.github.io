    The research conducted in the Materials Theory Group (at Yonsei University, South Korea) aims to understand the structural properties of materials (from metal to oxide, in particularly focusing on surface properties) and their impact to physiochemical properties. And I hope these fundamental knowledges can play a key role in their overall desired performance for scientific and industrial perspective.

    Following approaches make it possible to obtain these objectives. The main work horse is the density-functional theory by the combining the general concepts in solid-state physics.


    Theoretical approaches

    • Density-functional theory (DFT) and beyond, using Vienna Ab initio Simulation Package (VASP), DMol3
    • Ab initio molecular dynamics (aiMD) which is implemented in VASP code
    • Advanced ab initio atomistic thermodynamics and Gibbs-Wulff theorem
    • Theoretical core level binding energy (BE) and their shifts computation for the direct comparison with X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS) observation
    • Scanning tunneling microscopic (STM) simulations via bSKAN package -- first-principles simulation program of scanning tunneling microscopic data and spectroscopic spectra, developed by Dr. Krisztián Palotás and his colleagues)
    • Structural evolution and discovery with nature-inspired algorithms: CALYPSO (Crystal structure AnaLYsis by Particle Swarm Optimization; Structure prediction software using swarm intelligence) and Atomic Energy Network (ænet)

    These approaches make possible to predict accurate local atomic structures with their unique electronic structures. Furthermore, the structure−property relationship can be rationalized by examining how the structural displacement (e.g, inter- and intra-distortion of atoms, polymorphism or polytypism and structural reconstructions)can impact the electronic structures. On this basis, we can find the design rule for the high-performance devices for future key energy applications.

    In addition, I am interested in the growth mechanisms and heterogeneous catalysis process where the atomic-scale understanding of structural changes is important. Despite of receiving a tremendous amount of interest in those surface progress, there detailed surface mechanisms (at the atomic-scale) are still clearly lacking. Therefore, those theoretical surface characterization might be able to contribute to build the fundamental foundations and it can bring positive environmental impact, particularly in the area of clean energy technologies.
